Biography

My name is Elena Álvarez Mellado. I’m a Computational Linguistics graduate student at Brandeis University (Massachusetts). I’m interested in Linguistics, technology and the intersection between them. I currently work as Research Data Analyst on medical NLP at McLean Hospital.

I am also highly involved in activities that bridge the gap between Linguistics and the general public: I write a column about language at Spanish national newspaper eldiario.es, I serve as editorial board member of linguistics magazine Archiletras and I’m a proud member of the scientific committe behind Maricorners conference. In 2016 I wrote the pop linguistics book Anatomía de la Lengua.

Writing & dissemination

I write a column about Linguistics on Spanish national newspaper eldiario.es, a column that was awarded with the National Award of Journalism in 2017.

I also write for Archiletras, a pop Linguistics magazine where I’m also member of the editorial board.

From 2012 to 2015 I was a radio contributor at Spanish National Radio (RNE) on a weekly section about language and Linguistics.

In 2016 I wrote the pop linguistics book Anatomía de la Lengua.
